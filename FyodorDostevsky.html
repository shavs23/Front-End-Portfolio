Fyodor Dostoevsky

19th Century Russian Writer

  • 1821: Born on November 11, 1821 in Moscow.

  • 1837: Mother dies at the age of 15 and he joins Nikolayev Military Engineering Institute.

  • 1840: He enjoyed a lavish lifestyle as an engineer and earned extra income by translating books.

  • 1845: Wrote his first novel "Poor Folk" which gained entrance into St.Petersburg literary circles.

  • 1849: Arrested for his writing against the Tsarist Russia and given a death sentence.

  • 1854: Death sentence commuted at last moment and served sentence for four years at a Siberian prison camp until 1854.

  • 1860: Served another six years under compulsory military service in exile till 1860.

  • 1866: Became a journalist and wrote and edited several magazines and published a book, "A Writers Diary".

  • 1868: Developed a gambling addiction which in turn led to financial ruin and him begging for money.

  • 1870: Overcame his addiction and became one of the most widely read Russian author till date.
